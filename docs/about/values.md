# Values

These values are working standards. Their meaning is visible in decisions and behavior, not in the words alone.

## Integrity

Keep promises where possible, acknowledge broken commitments quickly, and restore workability rather than hiding failure.

## Source

Act as someone who can initiate a responsible next step instead of waiting for circumstance, authority, or blame to determine every action.

## Commitment Beyond Oneself

Organize work around a future or contribution larger than personal status, comfort, or short-term gain.

## Authenticity

Say what is actually known, observed, uncertain, wanted, or unfinished. Do not use confidence, expertise, or scientific language to cover a gap in evidence.

## Sustainability

Build systems, relationships, funding models, and working practices that can continue without consuming the people or resources on which they depend.

These principles are aspirations subject to evidence and revision. The important question is where the work embodies them, where it falls short, and what repair is required.
