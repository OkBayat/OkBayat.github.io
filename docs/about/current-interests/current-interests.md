# Current Interests

{ Last updated July 17, 2026 | sub }

My current interests are divided between two kinds of work: building quantitative software and organizations, and studying how people learn, remember, decide, and coordinate.

## Vocora and Human Learning

Through [Vocora](/building/vocora), I am currently studying:

- retrieval practice and delayed recall;
- spaced review and forgetting;
- vocabulary and spelling practice;
- feedback after errors;
- motivation, autonomy, and ethical gamification;
- the difference between activity, performance, and durable learning;
- privacy-conscious learning analytics;
- the role of context and pattern recognition in language learning.

The current goal is not to make broad claims about the brain. It is to turn a small number of questions into documented product decisions and, where possible, measurable tests.

## K2Quant and Company-Building

Through [K2Quant](/building/k2quant), I continue to work on:

- quantitative and market-related software systems;
- technical reliability and maintainability;
- research and decision-support tools;
- team coordination and operating systems;
- leadership under uncertainty;
- the relationship between technical decisions and organizational behavior.

## Broader Reading

I am also reading across cognitive science, learning science, philosophy, leadership, software architecture, artificial intelligence, and organizational design.

This page is intentionally current rather than permanent. Subjects may be added, narrowed, or removed as the work changes.
