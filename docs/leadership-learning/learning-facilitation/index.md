# Learning & Facilitation

This section examines facilitation as the design of conditions for learning—not permanent control of the learner or group.

## Practice Areas

- [Coaching](/work/leadership-learning/learning-facilitation/coaching) — the scope, competencies, ethics, and boundaries of my coaching practice.
- [Learning Circle](/work/leadership-learning/human-transformation/field-projects/learning-circle) — a field project about learner ownership, peer teaching, group coordination, and facilitator withdrawal.
- [Practice & Programs](/work/leadership-learning/human-transformation/practice-programs) — durable records of facilitated programs and their evidence limits.
- [Courses](/work/leadership-learning/courses) — current and historical course materials.

## Working Questions

- When does support increase agency, and when does it create dependence?
- What can a group continue doing after the facilitator steps back?
- How should feedback be given without turning reflection into surveillance?
- Which outcomes belong to the learning design, and which depend on selection, relationships, incentives, or the original setting?
- What consent is required before material from a learning relationship is used publicly?
