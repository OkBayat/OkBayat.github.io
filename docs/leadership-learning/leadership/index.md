# Leadership

Leadership is one of the principal settings in which I encounter questions about language, identity, context, responsibility, coordination, performance, and durable change.

This section preserves several years of programs, coaching material, translations, concept notes, and practice-based observations. It should not be read as a single validated theory or as evidence that every participant or organization will experience the same result.

## What Is Here

### [Leadership Programs](/work/leadership-learning/courses)

An archive of facilitated programs and historical course pages. Program history and participant accounts are separated from claims of efficacy and current enrollment.

### [Leadership Source Library](/work/leadership-learning/leadership/resources)

Source documents, translations, and concept pages. These pages should identify their original lineage and distinguish source claims from my interpretation.

Coaching and group facilitation are organized under [Learning & Facilitation](/work/leadership-learning/learning-facilitation), where their practice boundaries can be considered alongside other learning settings.

## Relationship to Human Transformation

Leadership is not the parent category for every question about human change. It is a practical domain inside the broader [Human Transformation](/work/leadership-learning/human-transformation) inquiry, alongside children's independent learning, language and memory tools, identity, group coordination, and quality of life.

Related published work and field records are collected in [Selected Research-Related Work](/research-practice/publications), while [Methods, Ethics & Evidence](/research-practice/methods-ethics-evidence) states the evidence and participant-protection boundaries applied to them.
