# Leadership & Learning Perspective

Leadership and learning meet in a practical question: what enables people to see a situation differently, coordinate responsibly, and continue acting when direction, pressure, or the original facilitator is absent?

My current perspective is guided by five principles:

1. **Context shapes action.** Skill and intention matter, but people also act inside language, relationships, histories, incentives, and shared interpretations.
2. **Learning is more than temporary performance.** A strong moment in a course or team is not yet evidence of durable transfer.
3. **Responsibility should expand agency.** Leadership should not make every decision dependent on one central person.
4. **The facilitator is part of the system.** Presence, authority, expectations, and evaluation can alter what participants say and do.
5. **Claims must match evidence.** Experience can generate insight and questions without proving a universal result.

This perspective is developed through [Leadership](/work/leadership-learning/leadership), [Human Transformation](/work/leadership-learning/human-transformation), [Learning & Facilitation](/work/leadership-learning/learning-facilitation), and the published work collected under [Selected Research-Related Work](/research-practice/publications).
