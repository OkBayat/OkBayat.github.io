# Thinking

This section contains written work at different stages and with different purposes. The label matters: an essay, a working note, and a translation should not be presented as the same kind of authorship or evidence.

## Sections

### [Essays](/thinking/essays)

Original long-form arguments, interpretation, and synthesis written by Mohammad Bayat.

### [Research Notes](/thinking/research-notes)

Working investigations, literature notes, design questions, and revisions. These pages may be incomplete and are not assumed to be peer reviewed.

### [Book Notes](/thinking/book-notes)

Notes and interpretations developed while reading a specific book.

### [Translations](/thinking/translations)

Translations and adaptations that identify the original author, source, and translator.

## Reading the Work

Research-related pages should distinguish among:

- evidence reported by a source;
- my current interpretation;
- a product or practical implication;
- limitations and uncertainty;
- questions that remain open.

The purpose is not to make every page sound final. It is to make the status of each idea visible.
