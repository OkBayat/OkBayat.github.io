# Research Notes

Research Notes contain work in progress: a question being clarified, evidence being reviewed, a design assumption being examined, or an interpretation being revised.

They are not automatically scientific papers and are not assumed to be peer reviewed. A strong note should make its status and uncertainty visible.

## Recommended Structure

1. Research question
2. Why it matters
3. Evidence reviewed
4. What the evidence suggests
5. Current interpretation
6. Implications for practice or a project
7. Limitations and uncertainty
8. Open questions
9. References

## Vocora

Notes connected to Vocora are also collected in [Vocora Publications & Notes](/building/vocora/publications). Each note remains here as its canonical page.
