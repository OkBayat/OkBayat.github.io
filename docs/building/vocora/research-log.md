### Clarified

- The current product is a vocabulary and spelling practice application, not a validated cognitive intervention.
- Same-session correctness, Leitner box state, and streak length are not treated as proof of durable learning.
- The current review schedule is functional but has not been shown to be optimal.
- Gamification decisions require learning or practice goals, privacy constraints, and guardrail metrics.
- Formal experiment reports will not be published until a question, method, metrics, comparison, and limitations are defined.

### Next Questions

- How should delayed spelling recall be measured?
- What baseline should be used to evaluate the current review schedule?
- How should meaning and context be added to isolated spelling practice?
- Which outcomes should be checked before adding streaks or social features?

## Log Standard

Future entries should record only substantive changes, including:

- a new or revised research question;
- a change in evidence or interpretation;
- a predeclared experiment protocol;
- a result, including a null or inconclusive result;
- a product decision made because of evidence;
- a correction to an earlier claim.
